Our Mission

To preserve, interpret, and promote Blacksburg's art, history, and cultural heritage through stewardship of the Alexander Black House and St. Luke & Odd Fellows Hall.

Our Story

The Blacksburg Museum is dedicated to sharing the rich history of our community. Through two historic properties, we tell the stories of Blacksburg's diverse residents and their contributions to the region.

The Alexander Black House, built in 1897, serves as our primary exhibition space and community gathering place. St. Luke & Odd Fellows Hall preserves the legacy of Blacksburg's African American community in the historic New Town neighborhood.

The Alexander Black House

Features rotating exhibitions, programs and events, children's room, gift shop, library and genealogy center, Alexander Black bedroom display, outdoor exhibitions, education and workshop room, staff offices, and much more!

St. Luke & Odd Fellows Hall

The last remaining New Town building, which was a neighborhood that grew amid a racially divided climate. Its residents didn't have the same resources as Blacksburg's white population, so they built a stable community in response to create meaningful lives for themselves.
